Why it happens

Parents are usually told:

“Just read more.”
“Practice sight words.”
“It will click eventually.”

But reading isn’t natural.

The brain has to build a mental word dictionary where spelling, sound, and meaning are permanently connected. Researchers call this process orthographic mapping.

Until that happens, reading feels like cracking a code on every single word.

The good news: once you know what the brain actually needs, you know exactly where to focus.
